# Challenge Rules

## The Mission
Design a **50×50×50 mm** lattice that can absorb the impact of a **6 kg weight** dropped from a height of **50 cm** (approximately 29.4 J of energy) without catastrophic failure. The goal is to make the design as **light as possible** while ensuring it survives the impact.

## Participants & Teams
*   Form groups of up to **four people**.
*   Documenting your design reasoning is **non-negotiable**; a "lucky guess" without a clear argument will not be considered for the win.

## Technical Constraints
*   **Envelope:** 50×50×50 mm.
*   **Material:** PA 12.
*   **Manufacturing:** Must be 3D printable.
*   **Design Freedom:** Any topology (lattice, TPMS, Voronoi, etc.), unit cell size, or spatial variation (graded density) is allowed.

## Deliverables (Due May 29, 2026)
1.  **STL File:** Watertight, within the 50 mm cube, printable as a single part.
2.  **Project Report:** Short, structured document detailing the problem understanding, design method, and predicted mass/response.

## Important Deadlines
*   **April 23, 2026 (Challenge Day):** End-of-day pitch outlining the planned approach and topology.
*   **May 29, 2026:** Final submission deadline for the STL file and report. *Late submissions forfeit the physical test.*
*   **Trondheim Session:** Project presentations and winner announcement.

## Evaluation & Winning Criteria

The evaluation will be based on a combination of **weight** and **mechanical performance under impact**.

* **Weight score:**  
  Each lattice will be weighed. Lighter designs will receive a higher score.

* **Mechanical response score:**  
  During the drop tower test, the force–displacement response will be recorded and used to derive the stress–strain curve.  
  The highest score will be assigned to lattices that:
  - effectively absorb the impact energy, and  
  - do **not** enter the densification regime.

* **Final ranking:**  
  The final score will be obtained by combining the weight score and the mechanical response score. The design with the highest total score will be declared the winner.

* **Additional considerations (tie-breakers):**
  - Energy absorption efficiency.
  - Quality of the design rationale in the report.
  - Clarity of the Trondheim presentation.
